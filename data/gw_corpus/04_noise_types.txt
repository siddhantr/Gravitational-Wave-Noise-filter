# Noise Types in Gravitational Wave Detectors

## Overview of Detector Noise

LIGO sensitivity is limited by many noise sources across different frequency bands.
Understanding these noise types is essential for designing filtering strategies.
Total noise PSD: S_total(f) = S_seismic + S_thermal + S_shot + S_QRPN + S_technical + ...

The characteristic "S-curve" (noise bucket) shape of LIGO ASD:
- Low frequency (< 20 Hz): seismic noise dominates
- Mid frequency (20-200 Hz): thermal noise dominates
- High frequency (> 200 Hz): shot noise dominates
- Best sensitivity: ~100-300 Hz

## Seismic Noise

### Sources
Ground motion from:
- Microseismic hump: ocean waves at 0.1-0.3 Hz (100-300 mHz)
- Human activity: traffic, industrial vibrations at 1-10 Hz
- Earthquakes: broadband, can saturate detector
- Wind: tree sways, building vibrations

Seismic noise spectrum: S_seis(f) ∝ f^(-4) to f^(-8) (very steeply falls with frequency)
At 10 Hz: ground moves ~ 10⁻¹⁰ m/√Hz
At 1 Hz: ground moves ~ 10⁻⁸ m/√Hz

### Isolation Strategy
Multi-stage pendulum: each pendulum stage reduces seismic above its resonance by (f_res/f)²
Active seismic isolation (HAM-ISI, BSC-ISI systems): measure ground motion → feed-forward cancellation
Hydraulic External Pre-Isolator (HEPI): 6-DOF active isolation, factor ~100 reduction
HAM-ISI: second stage, brings seismic noise down by factor >10,000 at 10 Hz

Residual seismic coupling: through acoustic, electromagnetic paths (not mechanical).

## Thermal Noise

### Brownian Motion / Thermal Noise
Fluctuation-dissipation theorem: any mechanical system with dissipation has thermal noise.
One-sided PSD: S_x(f) = (4 k_B T / 2π f) Im[χ(f)]
where χ(f) = mechanical susceptibility, k_B = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K, T = temperature

Thermal noise ~ 1/√f × √(loss angle): lower loss → lower thermal noise.

### Mirror Coating Thermal Noise (Dominant at 50-200 Hz)
Alternating high-index (Ta₂O₅) and low-index (SiO₂) dielectric coatings.
Loss angle of coatings: φ_coating ~ 10⁻³ to 10⁻⁴
Much noisier than substrate: φ_substrate ~ 10⁻⁷ (fused silica)

Mitigation: new coating materials (crystalline AlGaAs, silicon carbide), amorphous silicon.
LIGO O4: improved coatings reduced coating thermal noise by ~30%.

### Suspension Thermal Noise (10-50 Hz)
Pendulum and violin modes of suspension fibers.
Fused silica fibers: very low mechanical loss, φ ~ 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻⁸.
Pendulum resonance: f_pend ~ 0.6 Hz → isolation above resonance.
Violin modes: sharp resonances at ~500 Hz (fibers vibrating like violin strings).

### Thermoelastic Noise
Temperature fluctuations → mirror expansion/contraction → position noise.
Substrate thermoelastic: S_TE ∝ (αT)²/(κ C_v) × f^{-1/2}
α = thermal expansion coefficient, κ = thermal conductivity, C_v = heat capacity.

Mitigation: cryogenic operation (KAGRA, Einstein Telescope) — lowers T and changes α.

## Shot Noise

### Photon Statistics
Laser light consists of photons following Poisson statistics.
Number of photons in time interval Δt: N ~ P Δt / (hf)
RMS fluctuation: ΔN = √N (Poisson), giving phase noise: Δφ ~ 1/√N

Shot noise ASD: S_shot^{1/2}(f) = √(hf_laser / P_circ) in phase units.
Converting to strain: S_h^{1/2}(f) = (1/L) √(hf_laser c / (2πf)² P_circ)

Shot noise is white (flat) above ~few Hz; S_shot ∝ 1/P_laser.
Increasing laser power reduces shot noise.

### Quantum Radiation Pressure Noise (QRPN)
Photons carry momentum; fluctuating radiation pressure pushes test mass.
S_QRPN ∝ P_laser / f⁴ × 1/m²

QRPN and shot noise trade off: SQL sets minimum combined quantum noise.
Standard Quantum Limit: S_SQL(f) = (4 ℏ) / (m (2πf)²)

Below SQL: requires quantum optics tricks (squeezed light, variational readout).

### Squeezed Light
Heisenberg: ΔX₁ ΔX₂ ≥ 1/4 (for quadrature amplitudes)
Squeezed state: ΔX₁ < 1/2 with ΔX₂ > 1/2 (uncertainty redistributed)
Inject squeezed vacuum into antisymmetric port → reduce shot noise in measurement quadrature.
LIGO O3: 3 dB squeezing demonstrated.
Frequency-dependent squeezing (with filter cavity): reduce both shot noise and QRPN simultaneously.

## Quantum Noise

At fundamental level, quantum noise includes:
- Shot noise (readout quantum noise): limits high frequency
- Radiation pressure noise (back-action): limits low frequency
- Optomechanical correlations: can be exploited

Quantum Fisher information limits and Cramér-Rao bound set ultimate sensitivity.

## Technical Noise Sources

### Laser Frequency Noise
Laser frequency fluctuations couple to arm length difference via mismatch.
Specification: ~10 Hz/√Hz of free-running laser → suppressed to 10⁻⁶ Hz/√Hz with pre-stabilization.
Reference cavity: Pound-Drever-Hall locking to ultra-stable Fabry-Pérot cavity.

### Laser Intensity Noise
Power fluctuations couple to radiation pressure → differential arm motion.
Intensity noise stabilization: in-loop photodetector + AOM feedback.

### Control Noise
Servo systems to hold cavities on resonance introduce noise.
Angular control, length control servo noise floors.
Balance: enough gain to suppress noise, not so much it injects elsewhere.

## Glitches (Non-Gaussian Transients)

Glitches are short-duration noise transients that can mimic or obscure GW signals.
Glitch rate in O3 LIGO: ~1 per 30-100 seconds.
Duration: 0.01 ms to several seconds.

### Common Glitch Types
- Blip: short, symmetric burst at ~100 Hz. Unknown origin.
- Koi Fish: scattered light with specific morphology.
- Scratchy: broadband noise, electrical.
- Scattered Light: nonlinear coupling of seismic to optical path.
- Helix: scattering from moving optical element.
- Violin Mode: excitation of suspension fiber resonances.
- 60 Hz Power Line Harmonics: electromagnetic coupling.

### Environmental Coupling
Acoustic: sound waves deform beam tube → length change.
Magnetic: Schumann resonances at 8 Hz, 14 Hz, etc.
Cosmic rays: muons pass through test masses → impulse noise.
Radio frequency: AM/FM radio coupling to control electronics.

### Glitch Mitigation
Physical environment monitoring (PEM) sensors: microphones, magnetometers, seismometers.
Hardware injections: simulate GWs to test pipeline.
Noise subtraction: measure noise source → subtract from main channel (e.g., seismic subtraction).
Gravity Spy: ML classification of spectrograms to identify and veto glitch types.

## Noise Budget

A noise budget accounts for all known noise contributions vs total measured noise.
If noise budget matches total noise → all noise understood.
Unexplained noise ("excess noise") → ongoing investigation.

LIGO noise floor at design:
- 10 Hz: ~10⁻²² strain/√Hz (seismic dominated)
- 100 Hz: ~4 × 10⁻²⁴ strain/√Hz (thermal dominated)
- 300 Hz: ~10⁻²³ strain/√Hz (shot noise dominated)
